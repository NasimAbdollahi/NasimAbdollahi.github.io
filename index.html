About me

I would like to be recognized for possessing the ability to bring together unrelated concepts, ideas and functions to create new possibilities that results in successful outcomes for the benefit of humanity.

Know me more

I am a post-doctoral fellow at University of Toronto, working with WangLab team. I am also a Machine Learning Research Intern at Cyclica Inc., a data-driven drug discovery company based in Toronto. My current research is focused on graph-based machine learning algorithms that can better predict the proteins’ biological functions from their three-dimensional (3D) atomic structure. I love that I have a chance to work with two amazing and brilliant teams at University of Toronto and Cyclica Inc. on cutting-edge technologies. More importantly, I believe the project I am working on makes a lasting contribution to the society, as it promises to increase our ability to design novel medicines and respond to global pandemics. To learn more about my current work read my blog post.

I have PhD in electrical and computer engineering and M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Biomedical engineering. I have always been passionate about applying technologies in the health care domain. During my PhD, at UofM EMILab, I worked on developing novel 2D and 3D microwave imaging algorithms to enhance breast cancer imaging and tumor detection.

When I am not working, I love to spend time with family and friends. I am also passionate about art and love painting. Besides, I am an advocate for breaking bias, promoting women and inspiring girls to follow their academic interests to a career in STEM, and have been actively involved with IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE) for several years.
